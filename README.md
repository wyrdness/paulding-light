## Overview

The Paulding Light is a well-known 'ghost light' or 'spook light' that appears nightly in a valley off Robbins Pond Road (Old US 45) near the unincorporated community of Paulding in Ontonagon County, Michigan, in the state's Upper Peninsula. It typically appears as a yellow or whitish glow that brightens, dims, and changes colour over minutes. The first widely cited modern report dates to 1966, when local teenagers reported the light to a sheriff's deputy. Local folklore variously attributes it to the lantern of a railroad brakeman killed on long-vanished tracks, the ghost of a murdered mail courier, or other regional spirit-narratives. In 2010, members of the Michigan Technological University SPIE student chapter conducted a structured investigation using telescopes and observed automobile headlights and tail lights, an Adopt-a-Highway sign, and police flashers on a stretch of US Highway 45 approximately 4-5 miles (about 7-8 km) north of the viewing site. They reproduced the light's behaviour by driving a car through that stretch. The visibility of distant headlights from the viewing area is enabled by a stable temperature-inversion layer over the valley that creates an atmospheric optical duct. Despite the published explanation, the site remains a popular paranormal-tourism destination.
